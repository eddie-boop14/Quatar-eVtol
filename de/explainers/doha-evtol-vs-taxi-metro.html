Doha eVTOL vs taxi vs metro — comparison

On 17 November 2025, EHang's EH216-S pilotless eVTOL completed point-to-point human-carrying trial flights between Old Doha Port and Katara Cultural Village in 8 minutes, on a corridor stated by EHang to take 30 minutes by road. The Doha Metro covers a comparable journey (Msheireb–Katara) in about 15 minutes for QAR 2. Commercial eVTOL fares have not been published.

Überblick

This page compares three modes of transport on the same Doha corridor that EHang and Qatar's Ministry of Transport used for the country's first urban pilotless eVTOL trial: Old Doha Port ↔ Katara Cultural Village. The eVTOL data point is from EHang's investor relations announcement of 17 November 2025; metro data is from Qatar Rail and Visit Qatar; taxi data is from Mowasalat (Karwa).

Two important notes before the numbers. First, the eVTOL flight was a trial conducted with QCAA operational authorisation, not a commercial service — there is no published fare and no scheduled flight. Second, comparing the eight-minute flight against a 30-minute car trip is EHang's own statement; actual road travel time varies with traffic.

Anfahrt

Mit der Metro

Doha Metro Red Line — Msheireb (interchange near Old Doha Port) and Katara stations. Journey time approximately 15 minutes including transfer; QAR 2 standard single.

Mit dem Auto

Old Doha Port to Katara is approximately 12 km / 7.7 miles by road via Corniche Road. Karwa taxis are metered.

Vom Flughafen

Not applicable — Hamad International Airport (DOH) is in Qatar; this corridor is internal to Doha city.

Regulatorischer Status

The 17 November 2025 eVTOL flights took place under operational authorisation from the Qatar Civil Aviation Authority (QCAA), with strategic support from the Ministry of Transport. They were trial operations — no commercial air operator certificate has been issued for eVTOL passenger service in Qatar. The Doha Metro is operated by Qatar Rail under regulatory oversight of the Ministry of Transport. Karwa taxis are regulated by Mowasalat, the state-owned transport company, under the Ministry of Transport.

Aktueller Stand

As of May 2026, only metro and taxi service operate commercially on this corridor. The eVTOL data point is a single trial campaign. EHang has stated that future development will focus on shuttle services between airport and urban centres, air-taxi corridors serving tourism and high-traffic zones, and integration into Qatar's multimodal transport strategy — but no commercial launch date has been published by EHang or the Ministry of Transport.

Häufig gestellte Fragen

Is Doha eVTOL faster than the Doha Metro?
On the Old Doha Port to Katara corridor that EHang trialled in November 2025, the eVTOL completed the trip in 8 minutes versus approximately 15 minutes by Doha Metro (Msheireb to Katara). On longer corridors the gap could be larger, but Doha eVTOL service is currently trial-only and not commercially available.
How much does Doha eVTOL cost compared to a taxi?
Doha eVTOL fares have not been published. EHang's 17 November 2025 flights were trial operations under QCAA authorisation, not paid commercial service. By comparison, a Karwa taxi from Old Doha Port to Katara typically costs QAR 25-35 metered, with a QAR 7 minimum fare and QAR 1.60/km daytime rate.
Is Doha eVTOL cheaper than the Doha Metro?
Almost certainly not, when commercial service eventually launches. The Doha Metro charges QAR 2 for a standard single journey and QAR 6 for an unlimited day pass — among the lowest fares of any mass-transit system in the Gulf. eVTOL service worldwide has been positioned as a premium-priced product. EHang has not published Doha eVTOL pricing.
When will Doha eVTOL be available alongside the metro and taxis?
Qatar's Ministry of Transport has stated that commercial deployment of pilotless eVTOL aircraft will follow a phased path including infrastructure readiness, operational system approvals, and regulatory framework development. No commercial launch date has been published by EHang, the Ministry of Transport or the QCAA.
Does the Doha eVTOL trial replace the metro or taxi service?
No. The November 2025 EHang trial in Doha was framed by both EHang and the Ministry of Transport as a complement to the existing multimodal transport strategy, not a replacement. The Ministry of Transport's 2025-2030 strategy focuses on integration between transport modes.
What corridor was used for the Doha eVTOL trial vs. the metro and taxi?
The corridor was Old Doha Port to Katara Cultural Village — approximately 12 km by road. EHang flew it in 8 minutes; the same trip is approximately 30 minutes by car (per EHang's statement) and around 15 minutes on the Doha Metro Red Line via Msheireb.
Is Doha eVTOL safer than taxis or metro?
Direct safety comparison is not possible at this stage. The Doha eVTOL trial used the EH216-S, which holds the world's first type certificate, production certificate and standard airworthiness certificate for pilotless eVTOL aircraft from the Civil Aviation Administration of China (CAAC) — but no Qatar-specific commercial safety record exists. The Doha Metro and Karwa taxi services are mature operations.
