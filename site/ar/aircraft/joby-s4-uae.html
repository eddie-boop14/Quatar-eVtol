Joby S4 (UAE Operations)

The Joby S4 is the all-electric piloted air taxi being prepared for commercial service in Dubai under Joby Aviation's six-year exclusive RTA agreement. The first S4 was delivered to the UAE on 30 June 2025; on 9 November 2025 a piloted S4 completed the first crewed point-to-point eVTOL flight in the UAE between Margham and Al Maktoum International Airport (DWC), 17 minutes.

نبذة

The Joby S4 is the all-electric, piloted eVTOL air taxi developed by Joby Aviation of Santa Cruz, California. The aircraft has six tilting propellers, four lithium-ion battery packs, and is designed to carry one pilot and four passengers. Joby's published cruise speed is up to 200 mph (≈320 km/h) and the company's published range figure is 150 miles (≈241 km) on a single charge. The S4 is the only aircraft authorised under Joby's exclusive Dubai operating agreement with the Roads and Transport Authority (RTA).

In UAE service, the S4 is being operated under a parallel-track regulatory pathway: a General Civil Aviation Authority (GCAA) qualification programme for the aircraft, alongside Joby's GCAA Air Operator Certificate application (Letter of Intent submitted 9 September 2024). The GCAA Director-General has stated publicly that air-taxi certification is targeted for completion by Q3 2026. In parallel, Joby is in the final stages of FAA Type Certification under Part 21.17(b) for the powered-lift category; the first FAA-conforming aircraft began power-on testing in Marina, California on 5 November 2025.

الوضع التنظيمي

The Joby S4 is being qualified for UAE service under two parallel tracks. Joby submitted a Letter of Intent for an Air Operator Certificate to the General Civil Aviation Authority (GCAA) on 9 September 2024, beginning a five-stage AOC process. In parallel, the GCAA is running an aircraft qualification programme distinct from FAA Type Certification, working with Joby alongside the RTA, the Dubai Civil Aviation Authority (DCAA) and Dubai Air Navigation Services (DANS). The GCAA Director-General has publicly stated that air-taxi certification is targeted for completion by Q3 2026.

In the United States, the S4 is in Stage 4 of the FAA's five-stage Type Certification process for the powered-lift category under Part 21.17(b). The first FAA-conforming aircraft, registration N547JX, began power-on testing in Marina, California on 5 November 2025; Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) flight testing with FAA pilots is targeted for 2026.

التقدم الحالي

Joby's UAE flight test campaign through the end of 2025 comprised 21 piloted S4 flights at Margham over the summer (full transitions, vertical-to-wingborne-to-vertical), the 9 November 2025 piloted Margham → DWC point-to-point flight (17 minutes), and daily S4 demonstration flights during Dubai Airshow 2025 (17–21 November). Joby has stated that passenger-carrying flights in the UAE under the GCAA qualification programme are possible in advance of full FAA Type Certification, but no UAE passenger service date has been published. The targeted commercial launch remains 2026; Joby has not published a specific month.

الأسئلة الشائعة

What is the Joby S4 in UAE service?
The Joby S4 is the all-electric piloted eVTOL air taxi being prepared for commercial service in Dubai by Joby Aviation under a 6-year exclusive agreement with the Roads and Transport Authority (RTA). The S4 carries one pilot and four passengers, has six tilting electric propellers and is designed for cruise speeds up to 200 mph (~320 km/h) and a 150-mile (~241 km) range.
When did the first Joby S4 fly in the UAE?
Joby Aviation delivered the first S4 to the UAE and conducted the first piloted vertical-takeoff-and-landing wingborne flights in Dubai on 30 June 2025, witnessed by RTA Director-General H.E. Mattar Al Tayer. A summer 2025 campaign at the Margham flight test facility comprised 21 piloted S4 flights.
What was the first long-distance Joby S4 flight in the UAE?
On 9 November 2025 a piloted Joby S4 completed the first point-to-point eVTOL air taxi flight in the UAE, between the Margham flight test facility and Al Maktoum International Airport (DWC). The flight took 17 minutes and entered controlled airspace with the pilots communicating with ATC.
How many Joby S4 aircraft are in the UAE?
Joby Aviation has not published a specific count of S4 aircraft in the UAE. Joby's press releases through December 2025 refer to 'the aircraft' or 'its aircraft' (singular) — at least one S4 has been based at the Margham flight test facility.
Is the Joby S4 certified to carry passengers in the UAE?
Not yet for commercial passenger service. The Joby S4 is being qualified by the GCAA under a parallel-track qualification programme distinct from FAA Type Certification, while Joby's Air Operator Certificate application has been progressing since September 2024. The GCAA has publicly targeted air-taxi certification completion by Q3 2026. Joby has stated that UAE passenger flights under the GCAA qualification programme are possible in advance of full FAA Type Certification.
How loud is the Joby S4?
In NASA-cooperative testing, the Joby S4 measured 45.2 dBA in overhead cruise at 100 kt at 500 m altitude, and below 65 dBA during planned takeoff and landing measured at 100 m / 330 ft from the flight path. Joby has not published a 60 dB figure; the published numbers are from the May 2022 NASA acoustic test campaign.
Will the Joby S4 used in the UAE be a UAE-specific variant?
No. Joby has stated the same all-electric Joby S4 air taxi is the aircraft for UAE commercial service. The cabin is climate-controlled and the aircraft was validated for extreme heat through hot-weather trials at Edwards Air Force Base in California prior to UAE deployment.
What is the relationship between the Joby S4 and the Joby S4 hybrid-electric demonstrator?
They are separate programmes. The Joby S4 used in UAE commercial-readiness work is the all-electric production design. The Joby S4-T hybrid-electric demonstrator, developed in collaboration with L3Harris and first flown in Marina, California on 7 November 2025, integrates a gas-turbine generator and is positioned for potential defence applications. Only the all-electric S4 is referenced in Joby's UAE service plans.
