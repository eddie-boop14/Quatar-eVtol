About

The General Authority of Civil Aviation (GACA) is Saudi Arabia's civil aviation regulator, responsible for airworthiness, operational authorization, and aviation policy in the Kingdom. President is Abdulaziz A. Al-Duailej. EVP for Aviation Safety is Captain Sulaiman bin Saleh Al-Muhaimedi.

GACA published its Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) Roadmap in January 2024, providing the formal regulatory framework for eVTOL operations in the Kingdom. Key GACA actions to date:

  • 21 June 2023: Issued Special Flight Authorization for Volocopter NEOM test flight, after 18 months of pre-flight collaboration.
  • 22 January 2024: Published AAM Roadmap.
  • 12 June 2024: Supported EHang EH216-S Mecca Hajj flight alongside Ministry of Transport and Logistic Services, Ministry of Hajj and Umrah, and Ministry of Interior.
  • 22 July 2024: Signed MoU with Lilium at Farnborough Airshow on eVTOL regulatory development (counterparty Lilium dissolved Feb 2025).
  • 12 November 2025: Signed framework MoU with Joby Aviation.
  • 10 December 2025: Signed framework MoU with Archer Aviation.

GACA's regulatory posture has been collaborative and case-by-case to date; no standalone Saudi-specific eVTOL operational regulation has been published as a binding rule beyond the Roadmap framework.

Regulatory status

GACA is Saudi Arabia's civil aviation regulator. The AAM Roadmap published January 2024 provides the formal framework for eVTOL operations. Specific operational authorizations have been case-by-case (Volocopter NEOM, EHang Mecca). No standalone Saudi-specific eVTOL operational regulation has been published as a binding rule.

Current progress

As of 20 May 2026, GACA holds active framework MoUs with Joby and Archer. The Lilium MoU is effectively stranded post-insolvency. Saudi sandbox operations under THC+Red Sea Global partnerships with Joby and Archer are scheduled for H1 2026 — no Special Flight Authorizations published as of this date.

Frequently asked questions

What is GACA?
The General Authority of Civil Aviation (GACA) is Saudi Arabia's civil aviation regulator. President is Abdulaziz A. Al-Duailej.
Has GACA published an eVTOL framework?
Yes. GACA published its Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) Roadmap in January 2024, providing the formal regulatory framework for eVTOL operations in the Kingdom.
What has GACA authorized?
GACA issued the Special Flight Authorization for Volocopter's NEOM test flight on 21 June 2023. GACA also supported EHang's EH216-S Mecca Hajj flight on 12 June 2024 alongside other Saudi ministries.
Has GACA signed any eVTOL contracts?
GACA has signed framework MoUs (not binding contracts) with Lilium (22 July 2024, stranded post-insolvency), Joby Aviation (12 November 2025), and Archer Aviation (10 December 2025). MoUs are regulatory collaboration frameworks, not procurement deals.
How does GACA compare to UAE GCAA?
Both are national-level civil aviation regulators. GCAA has indicated air-taxi certification completion targeted for Q3 2026 and has published the first global hybrid-infrastructure framework. GACA has published an AAM Roadmap and authorized specific flights case-by-case, but has not yet announced a binding national eVTOL certification timeline.
