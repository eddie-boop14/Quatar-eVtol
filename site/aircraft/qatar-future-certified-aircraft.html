About

This is a placeholder entity for the future certified eVTOL aircraft that will operate commercial service in Qatar after the trial phase. As of 1 May 2026, no specific airframe has been named by any Qatar authority, operator, or airline.

Qatar's Ministry of Transport (MoT) stated on 15 November 2025 that the November 2025 trial 'will be carried out in several consecutive phases' and that the MoT 'will continue assessing the test results and lay down the regulatory and technical frameworks needed for approving and operating that type of mobility'. EHang's 17 November 2025 release names the intent to develop a 'strategic alliance' with the MoT but does not commit to a specific certified airframe for commercial operations. Separately, the Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) led BETA Technologies' US$318 million Series C funding round in October 2024 — a financial investment, not a Qatar deployment commitment.

Regulatory status

The Qatar Civil Aviation Authority (QCAA) and the Ministry of Transport have not published type-certification, validation, or commercial-operator framework documents for any post-trial eVTOL airframe. The MoT's 15 November 2025 statement explicitly notes that the regulatory and technical frameworks for commercial eVTOL operations are still to be laid down.

Current progress

As of 1 May 2026, no specific certified eVTOL aircraft has been named for Qatar commercial operations. The closest public-record linkage to a Western eVTOL OEM is the QIA-led BETA Technologies Series C of October 2024, which is a portfolio investment by Qatar's sovereign wealth fund and does not constitute a Qatar deployment commitment by BETA. No Qatar Airways partnership with Joby, Archer, Eve, Volocopter, Lilium or Vertical has been announced on operator IR or QCAA channels.

Frequently asked questions

Has Qatar's future certified aircraft for commercial eVTOL operations been named?
No. As of 1 May 2026, no specific Qatar future certified aircraft has been named by the Ministry of Transport, the Qatar Civil Aviation Authority, Qatar Airways, or any operator for commercial service. This entity is a placeholder for the airframe to be named at a later phase.
Will the EHang EH216-S become Qatar's future certified aircraft for commercial service?
Possibly, but no commitment has been made public. EHang's November 2025 release expresses intent to develop a strategic alliance with Qatar's Ministry of Transport, and the EH216-S is the only aircraft to have flown human-carrying trials in Qatar. Commercial certification as Qatar's future certified aircraft has not been announced by the QCAA.
Has Qatar Airways named a future certified aircraft partner among Joby, Archer, Eve, Volocopter or others?
No. As of 1 May 2026, Qatar Airways has not announced an eVTOL partnership with Joby Aviation, Archer Aviation, Eve Air Mobility, Volocopter, Lilium, Vertical Aerospace, or any other Western eVTOL manufacturer that would identify Qatar's future certified aircraft for commercial operations.
Is BETA Technologies Qatar's future certified aircraft via the QIA Series C investment?
No. The Qatar Investment Authority led BETA Technologies' US$318 million Series C funding round announced 31 October 2024. This is a financial investment by Qatar's sovereign wealth fund and does not name BETA as Qatar's future certified aircraft. No Qatar deployment of BETA's ALIA airframes has been announced.
When will Qatar select a future certified aircraft for commercial eVTOL service?
No selection date has been published for Qatar's future certified aircraft. The Ministry of Transport stated on 15 November 2025 that the regulatory and technical frameworks for commercial eVTOL operations would be defined in subsequent phases of the trial programme; no public timeline has been issued.
Will Qatar's future certified aircraft be autonomous like the EHang EH216-S?
The autonomy profile of Qatar's future certified aircraft has not been confirmed. The EH216-S used in November 2025 trials is fully autonomous (pilotless). Whether commercial-phase operations in Qatar will use autonomous, piloted, or remotely-piloted aircraft has not been published by the QCAA or the Ministry of Transport.
