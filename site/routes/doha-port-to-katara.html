Port of Doha ↔ Katara Cultural Village

On 17 November 2025, the EHang EH216-S completed the first urban human-carrying pilotless eVTOL flights in the Middle East between Port of Doha and Katara Cultural Village. The 8-minute flight replaces a ~30-minute drive. QCAA operational authorization; strategic support from Qatar's Ministry of Transport.

About

On 17 November 2025, the EHang EH216-S completed the first urban human-carrying pilotless eVTOL flights in the Middle East — short trial flights between the Port of Doha and Katara Cultural Village in Doha, Qatar. According to EHang's IR release, the 8-minute flight replaces a ~30-minute drive.

The flights received operational authorization from the Qatar Civil Aviation Authority (QCAA), with strategic support from the Ministry of Transport (MoT) under the framework of the Qatar National Vision 2030 and the MoT Strategy 2025–2030. Minister of Transport HE Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulla bin Mohammed Al Thani witnessed the trial flight on 15 November 2025 per the MoT release.

These were demonstration flights, not commercial service. No follow-on commercial operations have been announced.

Regulatory status

The flights received operational authorization from the Qatar Civil Aviation Authority (QCAA), distinct from the Ministry of Transport (MoT) which provided strategic policy support. The framework aligns with Qatar National Vision 2030 and MoT Strategy 2025–2030. As of 20 May 2026, no Qatar-specific eVTOL operational regulation has been published as a standalone document — QCAA's authorization was on a case-by-case basis.

Current progress

As of 20 May 2026, the 17 November 2025 trial flights remain the only urban-corridor eVTOL flights conducted in the Middle East. No follow-on commercial operations have been announced by EHang or QCAA.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Port of Doha ↔ Katara flight?
On 17 November 2025, the EHang EH216-S autonomous eVTOL conducted the first urban human-carrying pilotless eVTOL flights in the Middle East between Port of Doha and Katara Cultural Village, in 8 minutes versus ~30 minutes by car.
Was this a commercial flight?
No. These were demonstration flights, not commercial service. No follow-on commercial operations have been announced.
Who authorized the flight?
Operational authorization came from the Qatar Civil Aviation Authority (QCAA). Strategic support came from the Ministry of Transport (MoT) under the framework of Qatar National Vision 2030.
Is the EH216-S certified in Qatar?
The EH216-S holds a CAAC China type certificate (world's first autonomous eVTOL TC). QCAA authorized the Doha flights operationally on a case-by-case basis; no standalone Qatar-specific eVTOL operational regulation has been published as of 20 May 2026.
Will this route operate commercially?
No commercial service has been announced as of 20 May 2026.
