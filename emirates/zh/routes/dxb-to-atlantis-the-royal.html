DXB ↔ Atlantis The Royal

The DXB ↔ Palm Jumeirah route is one of the corridors in the initial Dubai eVTOL network being built by Joby Aviation, the Roads and Transport Authority and Skyports Infrastructure. RTA has stated the flight is expected to take approximately 10 minutes, compared with roughly 45 minutes by car. The route requires the Atlantis the Royal vertiport on Palm Jumeirah and the DXB vertiport — both under construction as of late 2025.

简介

The DXB ↔ Palm Jumeirah route is a planned eVTOL corridor between Dubai International Airport and Palm Jumeirah, anchored at the Atlantis the Royal vertiport. It is one of the corridors enabled by the initial four-vertiport network announced by the Roads and Transport Authority (RTA) and Joby Aviation: DXB, Dubai Mall, Atlantis the Royal, and American University of Dubai.

According to a statement by H.E. Mattar Al Tayer, Director General of RTA, carried via Joby's 17 November 2025 press release, the journey from Dubai International Airport to Palm Jumeirah is expected to take about 10 minutes by aerial taxi, versus roughly 45 minutes by car. The route does not yet exist in commercial form. As of May 2026, the DXB vertiport had reached structural completion and was scheduled for completion in Q1 2026; the Atlantis the Royal vertiport was at the announcement / planning stage.

如何到达

地铁

Dubai Metro Red Line serves Dubai International Airport (Airport Terminal 1 / Airport Terminal 3 stations). Palm Jumeirah is served by the Palm Monorail (independent of Dubai Metro), accessible via the Dubai Tram and Internet City / Damac Properties metro stations.

驾车

By road, DXB to Atlantis the Royal is approximately 35 km via Sheikh Zayed Road (E11) and the Palm Jumeirah trunk. RTA states the equivalent road journey takes roughly 45 minutes.

从机场出发

DXB Vertiport is at Dubai International Airport itself — the route originates here.

监管状态

Operations on the DXB ↔ Palm Jumeirah route will fall under the General Civil Aviation Authority (GCAA) for airworthiness and the Roads and Transport Authority (RTA) Dubai for the operational franchise, under Joby Aviation's 6-year exclusive agreement signed February 2024. The Dubai Civil Aviation Authority (DCAA) is also involved in the operational framework. As of May 2026, no specific route certification has been published — certification covers the operator and aircraft type at network level.

当前进度

Construction status of the two endpoint vertiports differs: the DXB vertiport is the most advanced and was reported by Skyports Infrastructure to be 60% complete as of November 2025, with Q1 2026 completion targeted. The Atlantis the Royal vertiport on Palm Jumeirah was at announcement / planning stage at that date. Commercial service on this corridor depends on both endpoints being delivered, plus operator and aircraft certification.

常见问题

How long will the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL flight take?
The Roads and Transport Authority of Dubai has stated that the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL flight is expected to take about 10 minutes, compared with roughly 45 minutes by car. This is according to a statement by H.E. Mattar Al Tayer, Director General of RTA, on 17 November 2025.
When will the DXB to Atlantis The Royal eVTOL route open?
The DXB to Atlantis The Royal eVTOL route is targeted for 2026, in line with the broader Dubai air taxi network commercial launch. A specific opening date has not been published. Both endpoint vertiports — DXB and Atlantis The Royal on Palm Jumeirah — must be completed and operational first.
Where does the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route start and end?
The DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route runs between the DXB vertiport at Dubai International Airport and the Atlantis the Royal vertiport on Palm Jumeirah. Both are part of the initial four-vertiport network announced by Joby Aviation and RTA Dubai.
Who operates the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route?
The DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route will be operated by Joby Aviation under the company's six-year exclusive air taxi agreement with the Roads and Transport Authority of Dubai, signed in February 2024.
What aircraft will fly the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route?
The DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route will use the Joby S4, an all-electric piloted aircraft carrying one pilot plus four passengers. The S4 is the only aircraft authorised under Joby's exclusive Dubai operating agreement during the six-year exclusivity period.
How much will the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL flight cost?
Pricing for the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL flight has not been published by Joby Aviation or the RTA. Several news outlets have referenced indicative figures, but no operator or regulator has confirmed an official fare.
Is the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route operational yet?
No. As of May 2026, the DXB to Palm Jumeirah eVTOL route is not operational. The DXB vertiport is under construction with Q1 2026 completion scheduled; the Atlantis the Royal vertiport is at planning stage.
