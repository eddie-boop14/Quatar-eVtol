Abu Dhabi Cruise Terminal Hybrid Heliport

Abu Dhabi Cruise Terminal Hybrid Heliport is the UAE's first hybrid heliport — an existing helipad being converted for dual conventional-rotorcraft and eVTOL use. GCAA design approval was announced on 23 April 2025 by Archer Aviation, with Falcon Aviation Services as the named infrastructure partner and Abu Dhabi Aviation as the eVTOL operator under the Launch Edition agreement.

About

The Abu Dhabi Cruise Terminal Hybrid Heliport is the UAE's first hybrid heliport — a conversion of an existing helipad to enable both conventional rotorcraft and eVTOL operations from the same facility. The GCAA design approval was announced on 23 April 2025 by Archer Aviation, marking what Archer's release called 'the first civil aviation authority globally to develop regulatory standards for hybrid infrastructure.'

The site is located at the Abu Dhabi Cruise Terminal, serving the Saadiyat Cultural District. The named infrastructure partner is Falcon Aviation Services (CEO Captain Ramandeep Oberoi). Design support was provided by Air Synapsis. The eVTOL operator is Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA), under its Launch Edition agreement with Archer Aviation.

The original completion target was H2 2025. As of 20 May 2026, no public completion announcement has been issued by Archer, Falcon, ADA or GCAA.

Regulatory status

The Cruise Terminal Hybrid Heliport is the first facility approved under the UAE's pioneering hybrid-infrastructure framework. Per Archer's 23 April 2025 release, 'the GCAA has become the first civil aviation authority globally to develop regulatory standards for hybrid infrastructure.' The full regulatory framework was on track for publication by July 2025 per the same release.

Current progress

As of 20 May 2026, the original H2 2025 completion target has slipped without a public completion announcement. The site has GCAA design approval and is in physical conversion, but no operational date has been confirmed by Archer Aviation, Falcon Aviation Services, Abu Dhabi Aviation, or GCAA.

Frequently asked questions

What is a hybrid heliport?
A hybrid heliport is a facility that supports both conventional rotorcraft and eVTOL operations from the same physical site. The Abu Dhabi Cruise Terminal site is the first such facility to receive design approval under the UAE GCAA's pioneering hybrid-infrastructure framework, announced 23 April 2025.
When will the Cruise Terminal Heliport open?
The original H2 2025 completion target has slipped without a public completion announcement as of 20 May 2026. Design approval was granted by GCAA on 23 April 2025; physical conversion is in progress.
Who is the named infrastructure partner?
Falcon Aviation Services is the named infrastructure partner. Note that Falcon is the infrastructure and heliport-management partner, not the eVTOL operator — the eVTOL operating role is held by Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) under its Launch Edition agreement with Archer.
Why does this facility matter?
Per Archer Aviation's 23 April 2025 release, this is the first facility approved under the world's first hybrid-infrastructure regulatory framework. It establishes a template for converting existing helipads to support eVTOL operations alongside conventional rotorcraft, accelerating eVTOL infrastructure rollout.
Will this serve as a primary commercial vertiport?
The Cruise Terminal Heliport is a hybrid facility, not a purpose-built commercial vertiport. Abu Dhabi's primary commercial vertiports are AUH and AZI (both under construction), with eight further sites planned in the 10-vertiport network announced November 2025.
